About Upside

The Upside is where purpose and optimism intersect.


We’re a group of passionate ladies committed to cultivating positive change - to creating the #upside when the downside seems insurmountable.

Our mission is to educate, inspire, and empower those around us to do the same…one event at a time.

We believe there’s never been a more critical time to consider what we stand for, to assert what we value, and to spark a conversation…. because positive change requires more than hope. It requires action, and of course, a little fun.

Anne-Marie Salcito

Equal parts rampant political facebook poster, ardent argument instigator and focused ad (wo)man at Karsh Hagen, Anne-Marie thrives on any opportunity to share and challenge perspectives and to foster understanding. Inspired by the brilliant, bold, impassioned women around her and driven by a desire to find and cultivate the “Upside” in a climate that made that possibility seem remote, Anne-Marie assembled this incredible group of ladies and continues to be uplifted by their endless ability to #slay.

Brianna Hernandez

Bri might be short and sweet, but she’s an absolute marketing badass at Booyah Advertising, and has spent the last decade fundraising and volunteering in Denver. From planning charity concerts to benefit Hurricane Sandy victims, to marching against School of the Americas, she is always eager to take action. When she’s not on a spin bike or hunting down the newest restaurant, she spends her time as a mentor for Minds Matter, is a member of Mile High Young Professionals, and is the Membership Co-Chair for CultureHaus at the Denver Art Museum. Inspired by the passionate and bold women of Upside, she joined to empower others to be agents of change.

Margaux Lochmiller

When Margaux isn’t busy slaying at Morgan Stanley Private Wealth Management, she slays at pretty much everything else she does. Including but not limited to: receiving scholarships and academic awards as a member of the University of Denver Division I Women’s Soccer Team, co-chairing the Third Way Center Young Professionals Board, and volunteering extensively across the country as well as internationally. As a Colorado native, Margaux enjoys the outdoors, mountaineering, yoga and the Broncos (duh), and her enthusiasm, experience, knowledge and expertise have been invaluable to helping bring the Upside vision to life.

Leslie Simonton

Leslie Simonton is a legend, among other things. She can golf circles around basically every dude we know and don’t even get us started on how she tears up the ski slopes on the weekends in winter. More importantly, Leslie is a strong, dedicated and brilliant force to be reckoned with both in the workplace and out. A native to Colorado Springs, she’s volunteered for the National Charity League and St Jude's Children's Hospital and currently works in commercial real estate. She joined Upside driven by her desire to spread knowledge and encourage her peers to follow in creating awareness and change to matters that need it most.

Jennifer Lazear

Ever heard the term “little sparkplug”? Google it. Now you know Jenn Lazear. Born and raised in Columbus, Ohio, Jenn has lived in Denver for 5 years, and we’re grateful to have her wit, her enthusiasm and her drive. She is a Commercial Real Estate Broker specializing in Retail Leasing and when she’s not focused on work she’s active in local AIPAC and Jewish Family Services initiatives as well as the International Council of Shopping Centers. Jenn joined Upside for the amazing opportunity to meet other like-minded women and provide fun programming for a great cause.

Rachael Greenberg

Rachael Greenberg, originally from Golden, Colorado is a marketing wizard at KPMG for her day job but her real work and her mark on this planet happens in the Denver community. She is a devoted mentor and trainer for Minds Matter Denver, a coach for City Lax Denver and is on the National Action Alliance for Suicide Prevention. Inspired by the genesis of Upside and the group of women saying yes to making change, she joined the team to help spread the message of taking action and questioning the status quo.

Bridget Rogers

You know those people you want to punch in the face bc everything they touch turns to gold? That’s Bridget. Eternal Michael Jackson mega-fan, founder of her event company, Cloth + Gold, and a devoted voice for positive change, she inspires those around her with her dedication, passion, and all around savvy. She was inspired to join Upside so that in 20 years when her kids ask her what she was doing during this time, she can say she made her voice loud and fought for what was right. Eff yes.

Grace Hartman

In addition to being the shit, Grace Hartman is a an avid fencer (she fenced D1 in college and coached in DC), as well as a UX Designer at Spire Digital. She's obsessed with all things visual—especially enhancing digital experiences—and has worked as a designer in Nepal, Brazil and all over the country. Experiencing new places, people, and cultures is what inspires her the most, so Grace is a travel fiend. While she's new to Colorado, we loved her IMMEDIATELY when she jumped at the opportunity to help craft a strong, smart and optimistic message + visual identity for Upside to help propel some positive change in this world. We’re forever grateful that Grace joined us.
